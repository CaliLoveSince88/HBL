TRANSPORTING CARGO WORLD WIDE

Harbor Logistix is dedicated to providing the highest quality logistic transportation services to businesses and individuals around the globe. 

Land Freight

Trucking is the primary mode of transportation for freight shipments in the state of California. Trucks transport the largest amount of goods by weight, both within the state and for imports and exports. In 2015, a staggering 911 million tons of freight, valued at $1.2 trillion, were transported over California's highway system by trucks. Specifically, in the Southern California region, approximately 800 trucking companies operate at the Ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach, utilizing around 16,000 trucks. However, the trucking industry in California is actively working towards a transition from diesel-powered equipment to more energy-efficient technologies. The latest heavy-duty vehicles on the market today are an impressive 99 percent cleaner than models from 1970, showcasing the commitment to sustainability and reducing environmental impact within the industry.

Air Freight

The state of California is an ideal location for major air cargo hubs, thanks to the vast size of the Southern and Northern California markets. In the north, Oakland Airport leads the domestic cargo traffic, while San Francisco Airport remains dominant in international cargo traffic. The Sacramento region is served by Sacramento and Mather airports. Similarly, in Southern California, Los Angeles and Ontario airports handle the bulk of air cargo, with San Diego Airport competing for the southern market share. The state's air cargo market is largely controlled by integrated carriers, such as FedEx, UPS, and DHL. However, Amazon has a significant presence in California, with a center located near the Sacramento Airport. Recently, Amazon has expanded its service to the Stockton Airport in the San Joaquin Valley, further solidifying its position in the state's market.

Sea Freight

California is home to the most efficient and productive network of ports in the United States. Among these ports are three of the largest container ports in the country, as well as a range of specialty ports. The ports in California play a crucial role in the state's economy and job market, contributing to about one-third of its overall economic activity. When it comes to containerized cargo entering the United States, over 40 percent of it arrives through California ports. Additionally, these ports handle almost 30 percent of the country's exports. The Port of Los Angeles holds the top position in national container volume, closely followed by the Port of Long Beach, making them the largest container port complex in the U.S. Not only are California ports at the forefront of the country, but they are also leading globally in terms of developing environmentally friendly port operations. Since 2005, the largest ports in California have reported significant reductions in emissions, with particulate matter decreasing by approximately 80 percent, Sulphur Oxides (SOX) by 90 percent, and Nitrogen Oxides (NOX) by 50 percent..

Rail Freight

- [ ] California has an extensive and well-connected rail system that is primarily served by two Class 1 railroads, BNSF and Union Pacific. Covering the entire state, these companies link up the ports with inland destinations within California and beyond. With over 2,000 track miles in California, BNSF is the largest intermodal carrier in the United States. Meanwhile, Union Pacific boasts an extensive network of nearly 3,292 track miles within the state. Additionally, short-line rail companies, which operate over 1,600 route miles across the state, handle a diverse range of cargo such as agricultural products, imported goods, lumber, and petroleum. These short-line railroads are an integral part of the communities in which they operate. Freight trains account for about 43 percent of the movement of goods between cities in California. In 2017, railroads moved a massive 162.3 million tons of freight either within or through the state. Shipping cargo by rail is not only safe but also cost-effective and environmentally friendly. The 2018 California State Rail Plan lays out a long-term vision for prioritizing the investment in a highly efficient freight rail system.
