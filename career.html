Truck Driver

  • End Date 12 Januray 2024
  • Los Angeles, California
  • Mid Level

Role Summary

A logistics truck driver is responsible for transporting goods from one location to another using a commercial vehicle, usually a truck or a tractor-trailer. This job typically involves the following responsibilities: 1. Vehicle maintenance: Before and after each trip, the driver must inspect the vehicle to ensure that it is in good working condition. This includes checking oil and fuel levels, testing brakes and lights, and performing other safety checks. 2. Load and unload cargo: The driver is responsible for loading and securing cargo properly so that it does not shift or become damaged during transport. 3. Plan routes: The driver must select the best route for the delivery, taking into account factors like traffic, road closures, and weather conditions. 4. Follow regulations: Drivers must follow all relevant federal and state regulations regarding the operation of commercial vehicles, including work restrictions, rest periods, and weight limits. 5. Maintain delivery records: The driver must keep accurate records of deliveries, including bills of lading, delivery confirmations, fuel receipts, and other documentation. 6. Communication: The driver must communicate effectively with dispatchers, customers, and other stakeholders to ensure that all parties involved are informed about the status of the delivery and any issues that arise. Logistics truck driver positions typically require a commercial driver's license (CDL), as well as a clean driving record and experience with driving larger vehicles. This job can be physically demanding and may require long hours on the road, and drivers must be able to handle stress and work independently.

Warehouse Officer

  • End Date 12 Januray 2017
  • Long Beach, California
  • Mid Level

Role Summary

A warehouse officer is an important role in a logistics company responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of a warehouse. The following are the responsibilities of a warehouse officer: 1. Receiving and Dispatching goods: The warehouse officer is tasked with receiving goods delivered by suppliers, confirming deliveries against purchase orders, and coordinating the dispatch of goods to customers or transportation companies. 2. Inventory management: Maintaining accurate inventory records through the use of an inventory management system ensuring the physical count is accurate and consistent with electronic inventory records. 3. Facility Maintenance: Ensuring that the warehouse and equipment meet required safety standards, recording any equipment faults and taking appropriate actions to address them through contractor management, repair or replacement. 4. Shipping and Packaging: Oversee the packaging of goods ensuring they meet required standards and accurately labelled. Liaising with courier and transportation companies to ensure successful distribution and delivery of goods. 5. Warehouse Security: Coordinating regular security checks, managing authorized personnel access, and ensuring that security systems are in place. 6. Reporting: Preparing reports and statistics regarding the operations in the warehouse, including analysis of the warehousing activities, identifying areas of improvement and recommending solutions. Other duties of a warehouse officer may include supervising staff, coordinating with customer service and all other internal departments as needed, and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements. To become a warehouse officer in a logistics company a bachelor’s degree in logistics, business management, or a related field is preferred. However, experience in warehouse operations, freight forwarding or logistics is a significant advantage. Strong organizational and communication skills are essential, as well as the ability to work well in a team.
